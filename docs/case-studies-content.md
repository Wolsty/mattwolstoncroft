## 1. HelloEyes iOS & Android Mobile Apps

**Thumbnail label**: IOS & ANDROID APPS / HELLOEYES

### Overview
HelloEyes is a first of its kind mobile vision platform that transforms your smartphone into a comprehensive virtual vision center, offering at home vision exams, prescription tools, and seamless eyewear ordering, all powered by AI and conversational design. Built for accessibility, speed, and trust, the app helps users manage their eye health without stepping into a clinic.

### My Role
- **Product Design & UX Optimization** — Led the design of critical flows across iOS and Android, including account creation and post-exam order pairing, to reduce friction, boost conversion, and reinforce trust.
- **Research-Driven Redesign** — Conducted usability testing and funnel analysis to identify and solve onboarding drop-offs. Redefined the account creation experience with real-time feedback, password strength indicators, and progressive disclosure, resulting in a 22% lift in successful sign-ups.
- **Journey Mapping & Conversion Strategy** — Identified a key post-exam blind spot and designed an in-app prescription pairing flow with smart nudges and contextual CTAs. This solution increased Rx-to-order attachment by over 50% and addressed a major abandonment issue.
- **Platform Leadership** — Shaped the mobile experience to reflect HelloEyes' core principles of accessibility, speed, and trust, positioning the app as a first-of-its-kind virtual vision center.

### Flows covered

**Order Pairing Flow** — Eliminate friction between obtaining a digital Rx and applying it to a product order. Solved a critical post-exam drop-off: users not knowing how to apply their new Rx to an eyewear purchase. Mapped the blind spot and designed a frictionless in-app mechanism to surface eligible orders and pair the prescription directly. Result: reduced Rx abandonment and improved attachment rate by **over 50%**.

**Lens Scanner Tool** (Landing Page, Tool Flow, FAQs) — Enable users to retrieve their glasses prescription from home. Proprietary feature that lets users scan their existing glasses to extract their prescription. Designed a benefits-first landing page, intuitive setup walkthroughs, and FAQ content informed by real-time support inquiries. Integrated user testing at every stage (calibration through results). Result: reduced task abandonment by **38%**. Became one of the highest-engaged features, especially for returning customers.

**PD Reader (Pupillary Distance Tool)** — Empower users to self-measure PD for accurate glasses fitting. Designed a computer vision–assisted flow that guides users through capturing their PD using their phone and a credit card for scale. Focused on trust building through transparent measurement logic, real-time visual feedback, and simplified instructions. Result: **33% increase** in successful PD captures and a **27% decrease** in order returns due to fit issues.

**Account Creation** — Simplify onboarding and reduce barriers while maintaining security and trust. Complete redesign to reduce drop-offs and align with HIPAA-grade security standards. Identified cognitive load and unclear password requirements as friction points. Implemented real-time validation, visual password strength indicators, and progressive disclosure. Result: **22% lift** in successful account creation.

**Password Reset Flow** — Ensure users can recover access quickly and confidently. Mapped the end-to-end recovery journey from reset request through reauthentication. Enhancements: clear, empathetic microcopy and inline error validation for real-time feedback. Result: **40% increase** in successful reset completions and a **32% drop** in login-related support tickets.

**Checkout & Payment for Vision Exam** — Minimize cart abandonment and build confidence in paying for a novel health service. Barriers uncovered: unclear value proposition, limited payment options, vague refund policies. Redesign added credibility (testimonials, guarantees), modern payment methods, and clarified post-purchase steps. Result: reduced checkout abandonment and increased conversion by **22%** within the first two release cycles.

## 2. Diary Study — Large Scale Usability Study

**Thumbnail label**: LARGE SCALE USABILITY / STUDY

### Overview
Over the course of several months, we ran a large scale diary study to understand the usability, stability, and overall satisfaction of our glasses ordering experience. Our goal was to uncover friction points across different parts of the journey, particularly with newer tools like our Vision Exam and Lens Scanner.

### My Role
- **Research Strategy & Execution** — Led the design and implementation of a multi-month diary study to evaluate the end-to-end glasses ordering experience, focusing on usability, trust, and tool performance.
- **Insight Synthesis** — Analyzed longitudinal feedback to uncover key friction points, particularly around the Vision Exam and Lens Scanner, and identified areas of strength in the shopping and prescription upload flows.
- **Product Impact** — Translated findings into actionable updates across product, content, and engineering, influencing redesign priorities, stability improvements, and help content expansion.

### Key Insights
- **Vision Exam Confusion** — Several users expressed uncertainty about the Vision Exam's credibility compared to traditional exams.
- **Lens Scanner Limitations** — Technical hiccups in the Lens Scanner tool led to frustration and drop off for a small subset of users.
- **Shopping Flow Strength** — Participants responded positively to the frame discovery experience, citing ease of browsing and filtering options.
- **Prescription Upload Simplicity** — Users who uploaded an existing prescription found the process straightforward and frictionless.

### Outcomes
- Redesign efforts were prioritized for the Vision Exam flow to better educate users upfront.
- Lens Scanner tool stability was improved based on technical feedback.
- Minor copy and layout updates were made to the shopping and checkout experience.
- Help content and FAQs were expanded to proactively address common user questions.

## 3. Online Vision Assessment

**Thumbnail label**: ONLINE / VISION ASSESSMENT

### Overview
The Vision Assessment was designed as an entry point into the HelloEyes ecosystem — a free, guided quiz-like experience that helped users navigate their vision needs and matched them with the right tools, styles, and services. Launched from the homepage, it acted as a digital concierge for our broader experience.

Under the surface, I strategically structured the Vision Assessment to surface qualified candidates for our AI powered vision exam, without promoting it broadly. The exam had strict eligibility requirements, and prior testing revealed user frustration when disqualified. This flow allowed us to deliver value to all users while quietly identifying and routing high-fit candidates to the exam experience. It increased product discovery, reduced bounce, and provided a more inclusive starting point for new users.

### The Challenge
The online vision exam was one of HelloEyes' most promising offerings but with one big caveat: strict eligibility rules. Many users who clicked into it were met with rejection, causing frustration and a sharp drop in trust. We had to find a better way to introduce the experience to the right users without alienating the rest.

At the same time, we noticed an opportunity on our homepage. Bounce rates were high. New users weren't sure where to start. We needed an engaging, frictionless entry point into our ecosystem that could deliver value quickly and build confidence in our brand.

### My Role
- **Experience Strategy** — Positioned the assessment as a welcoming front door to our broader product ecosystem, something for everyone, not just a gatekeeper to the exam.
- **Flow Architecture & Content Design** — Architected the logic and branching paths to ensure the experience was smooth, helpful, and tailored. Users felt guided, not filtered.
- **Outcome-Oriented Design** — Every screen was crafted with the dual purpose of delivering value to the user and surfacing actionable data for the business.

### Design Approach

1. **Guided Personalization** — Framed the assessment as a concierge-style guide. Users answered short, friendly questions about their vision history, current eyewear, goals, and lifestyle. Based on their answers, we offered curated recommendations — from trying on glasses at home to exploring contacts or scheduling an in-person exam.

2. **Invisible Qualification Logic** — Behind the scenes, layered in logic to identify high-fit candidates for the online vision exam. Rather than asking directly, we inferred eligibility through branching questions. When users did qualify, we introduced the exam as a tailored next step. This preserved the feeling of relevance, rather than rejection.

3. **Universal Value Delivery** — Every user, regardless of qualification, received personalized results and recommendations. This ensured no one left empty-handed, reinforcing trust and helping users feel seen.

4. **Seamless Integration** — Launched the assessment from multiple points across the site, including the homepage, product pages, and email flows. It became a flexible touchpoint that adapted to different user intents.

### Impact
- **100% Value Rate** — Every user who completed the assessment received personalized, actionable results, boosting satisfaction and creating a clear next step.
- **+40% Increase in Qualified Exam Leads** — By routing only high-fit users to the exam, we improved conversion rates and protected NPS from the drop-off previously caused by disqualification.
- **-30% Reduction in User Frustration** — By removing direct promotion of the exam as a "first touch," we avoided disappointment and confusion for users who wouldn't qualify.
- **Improved Homepage Engagement** — Users who completed the assessment were significantly more likely to explore tools, browse frames, and engage with services, making it a key driver of product discovery.

### Conclusion
The Vision Assessment wasn't just a quiz — it was a UX strategy disguised as a conversation. By embedding qualification logic into a helpful and empathetic experience, we turned what could have been a gatekeeping tool into a welcoming guide. It became the bridge between users' vision goals and HelloEyes' full range of offerings, balancing business outcomes with trust, clarity, and value at every step.

## 4. HelloEyes AI-Powered Vision Exam

**Thumbnail label**: AI-POWERED VISION EXAM / HELLOEYES

### Overview
The HelloEyes Vision Exam is a mobile-first, AI-driven experience that transforms any smartphone into a clinically validated vision center, enabling users to receive a doctor-issued vision prescription from the comfort of home, with no appointment needed. This proprietary flow integrates voice interaction, AI, and biometric calibration to deliver a guided exam that meets regulatory standards for prescription eyewear, redefining access to vision care through technology.

### My Role
- **UX Strategy & Research** — Defined the end-to-end user journey for a digital vision exam, grounding design decisions in usability testing, interviews, and behavioral data.
- **Product Design** — Designed intuitive flows for prescription confirmation, vision correction tasks, and eligibility screening to maximize clarity and compliance.
- **Clinical Collaboration** — Partnered with optometrists and regulatory teams to ensure clinical validity and meet telehealth standards while maintaining a user-friendly experience.

### Challenge
HelloEyes introduced an AI-powered mobile application enabling users to obtain vision prescriptions through voice interaction. Despite its innovative approach, the vision exam setup process faced significant user drop-offs due to:

- **Late-stage disqualifications** — Users discovered ineligibility (based on age, location, or time since last exam) only after completing most of the setup.
- **Lengthy instructional videos** — A 43% drop-off was observed between the first (1:15) and second (0:33) videos.
- **Environmental setup issues** — Users struggled with finding suitable environments and faced challenges resuming interrupted setups within a strict 30 minute window.
- **Technical glitches** — Issues like device instability, unclear phone angle guidance, and an overly sensitive lighting gauge led to frequent exits.
- **Permission hesitancy** — Users were reluctant to grant necessary permissions due to unclear justifications.

### Research Approach
- **Data Analysis** — Reviewed user behavior metrics to identify drop-off points.
- **User Interviews** — Engaged with a diverse user base to gather qualitative feedback.
- **Usability Testing** — Observed users navigating the setup flow to pinpoint friction areas.

### Key Findings
- Users felt frustrated upon learning about disqualification late in the process.
- Long videos were disengaging, leading to early exits.
- The inability to pause and resume the setup deterred users with environmental constraints.
- Technical instructions lacked clarity, causing setup errors.
- Permission requests lacked context, leading to user distrust.

### Design Solutions
- **Pre-Qualification Questionnaire** — Introduced a brief eligibility check before app download to filter out ineligible users early.
- **Instructional Content Optimization** — Replaced lengthy videos with concise, engaging content. Added a 'skip' option for experienced users.
- **Enhanced Resume Functionality** — Extended the setup window beyond 30 minutes. Implemented persistent reminders to encourage completion.
- **Improved Setup Guidance** — Provided real-time visual cues for phone positioning. Calibrated the lighting gauge to be more forgiving.
- **Transparent Permission Requests** — Included clear explanations for each permission, linking them to exam accuracy.

### Results
- **Completion Rate** increased by **30%**, surpassing the 25% target within three months.
- **Video Engagement** — Drop-off rates decreased to 20% with shorter content.
- **Resume Functionality** — 25% more users completed the setup after interruptions.
- **Setup Errors** — Device instability-related drop-offs reduced by 15%.
- **Permission Acceptance** — Improved by 18% due to clearer explanations.
- **Qualification Drop-offs** — Reduced by 35% through early eligibility checks.

### Conclusion
By addressing key pain points through user-centered design and rigorous testing, the HelloEyes vision exam setup flow became more intuitive and efficient. This project exemplifies my commitment to enhancing user experiences through strategic research and design interventions.

## 5. CreditRepair.com App Design

**Thumbnail label**: CREDITREPAIR.COM

### Overview
The CreditRepair.com mobile app was redesigned to give users more transparency, control, and confidence in their credit repair journey. As the lead product designer, I spearheaded the research, UX strategy, and UI design to create an intuitive, mobile-first experience that helps users stay informed and motivated as they work toward better credit.

### My Role
- Led end-to-end product design for iOS and Android
- Conducted qualitative user interviews and usability tests
- Analyzed behavioral data to identify friction points and drop-off
- Created wireframes, prototypes, and final UI designs
- Collaborated with product, engineering, and legal/compliance teams
- Delivered a scalable, responsive design system for long-term use

### The Challenge
Credit repair can be complex and confusing — especially for users already feeling overwhelmed by financial stress. The legacy app offered limited functionality, lacked personalization, and failed to communicate progress in a meaningful way. Users needed more than just access; they needed clarity, support, and motivation to stay engaged with the process.

### Research & Insights
Through user interviews and in-app behavioral data, we uncovered key pain points:
- **Lack of Progress Visibility** — Users didn't understand how disputes were progressing or what steps were being taken on their behalf.
- **Unclear Next Steps** — There was confusion around what users should do after signing up, leading to drop-off.
- **Poor Onboarding and Login Experience** — The app had outdated security flows, including a clunky login experience that caused frustration.

These insights directly informed our feature prioritization and UX strategy.

### Key Features & Design Improvements
- **New Personalized Dashboard** — Service-level-based dashboards clearly communicate what actions are being taken and what's next.
- **Biometric Login & Account Recovery** — Added Face ID and fingerprint login, plus streamlined account setup and recovery.
- **Score Tracker & Game Plan** — Visual tools that help users track their credit score and understand the factors impacting it.
- **Real-Time Progress Updates** — Users can view bureau disputes, creditor interventions, and total items removed — bringing transparency to a previously opaque process.
- **Alerts & Notifications** — Credit alerts and progress updates keep users informed and engaged.

### Outcome
The redesigned app helped reframe credit repair as a transparent, guided process rather than a black box. Early post-launch metrics showed:
- Increased app retention among new users
- Higher engagement with personalized features like score tracking and dispute updates
- Improved app store ratings due to smoother onboarding and more actionable insights

### Conclusion
The CreditRepair.com app redesign gave users the tools and confidence to take control of their credit journey. By grounding the experience in user research and real data, we created a mobile app that not only supported business goals — but genuinely helped people make progress toward financial stability.

## 7. Lexington Law iOS & Android Applications

**Thumbnail label**: LEXINGTON LAW iOS & ANDROID

### Overview
The Lexington Law Native App was designed to put the power of credit repair directly into users' hands. As the lead product designer, I was responsible for creating an experience that helped people understand, improve, and protect their credit from their mobile devices. The app serves as a personal credit advocate, combining legal expertise, real-time credit monitoring, and user-friendly tools to demystify a historically complex process.

### My Role
- **User Research & Insights** — Conducted user interviews, usability testing, and behavioral analysis to uncover pain points and identify opportunities to simplify the credit repair journey.
- **UX & UI Design** — Designed intuitive navigation, clear progress tracking, and mobile-first features like dispute submission, credit alerts, and educational modules.
- **Prototyping & Testing** — Built interactive prototypes to validate design concepts and iterate based on user feedback.
- **Stakeholder Alignment** — Collaborated closely with compliance teams to ensure legal accuracy, while advocating for the best user experience.

### Designing a Trusted Credit Repair Companion
I led the end-to-end design of the Lexington Law native mobile app, transforming a traditionally opaque credit repair process into a transparent, user-centered experience. The app was created to help people repair, monitor, and protect their credit using a combination of expert legal advocacy and intuitive mobile tools. My focus was on delivering clarity, guidance, and confidence to users navigating one of the most stressful aspects of their financial lives.

With a strong foundation in consumer protection law, Lexington Law helps users challenge inaccurate, unfair, or unsubstantiated credit report items across all three major bureaus. My task was to translate this legal complexity into an experience that felt approachable and empowering from a mobile device.

### Understanding the Problem Space
To start, I conducted user interviews with clients facing a range of credit challenges, from identity theft to divorce and medical debt. I mapped common emotional and functional pain points across the credit repair journey and used these insights to guide the structure and flow of the app. Key user goals included tracking dispute progress, understanding FICO® scores, and receiving alerts about credit activity.

### Key Features and Solutions

**Dispute Progress Tracking** — I designed a clear and modular view of each credit item being challenged. This included plain language explanations, legal context, and real-time status updates to reduce uncertainty and increase user trust.

**FICO® Score Insights** — For service tiers that included a TransUnion FICO® Score, I created visualizations to help users track score changes and understand the impact of key events on their credit.

**Focus Tracks** — I developed an intake flow that personalized the user's dispute strategy based on their life events. This feature aligned with the firm's legal approach and helped tailor communications with credit bureaus and creditors.

**Credit Monitoring and Alerts** — I introduced ReportWatch, a suite of alerts that kept users informed about credit activity and potential risks. I worked closely with engineering to ensure alerts were timely, relevant, and non-intrusive.

**Identity Theft Protection** — I collaborated with the legal and compliance teams to design a secure identity monitoring hub. This section of the app provided up to $1 million in insurance coverage and surfaced actionable protection tips.

**Digital Money Manager** — To extend value beyond credit repair, I designed a lightweight personal finance dashboard. Users could track their income, spending, budgets, debt, and net worth in one place. This feature empowered users to make smarter financial decisions post-repair.

**Case Setup Flow Optimization** — This project focused on streamlining the Case Setup experience within the Lexington Law app, a critical step where users provide information to begin the credit repair process. The original experience required users to complete similar sets of questions three separate times — once for each credit bureau, which led to unnecessary friction and drop-off. To solve this, I redesigned the flow to consolidate inputs into a single, intelligent step. Users now answer setup questions once, and logic within the system automatically maps those responses to the requirements of Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. This not only reduced cognitive load but significantly shortened task time. I incorporated conditional logic, clear progress indicators, and tailored microcopy to build confidence throughout the flow. Edge cases and error states were redesigned for clarity and recovery. The result was a smoother, more user-friendly experience that improved conversion through setup and reduced support burden related to bureau confusion. This overhaul reflects a broader effort to treat legal and financial workflows with consumer-grade design thinking — making a complex, multi-stakeholder process feel intuitive and seamless.

### Results and Impact
- Increased daily active use among paying clients by **27%** within the first three months of launch
- Reduced support ticket volume by **21%** by improving clarity across the dispute and score tracking interfaces
- Increased enrollment in identity protection and monitoring services by **18%** due to improved feature visibility
- Received strong qualitative feedback from users who felt "more in control" and "less overwhelmed" by their credit journey

## 8. Credit.com Platform & ExtraCredit Launch

**Thumbnail label**: CREDIT.COM

### Overview
At Credit.com, I led user experience and product design initiatives aimed at helping consumers take control of their credit and finances. From core products like the free credit score and Credit Report Card to the launch of a premium membership tier — ExtraCredit — I focused on building clear, actionable experiences that empowered users to improve their financial standing with confidence.

### My Role
- Led UX and product design across Credit.com's web and mobile platforms
- Conducted user research, interviews, and usability testing
- Translated behavioral analytics into product insights and roadmap priorities
- Designed the end-to-end experience for the launch of ExtraCredit
- Collaborated with cross-functional teams across product, growth, content, and engineering
- Built design systems and documentation to support scalable design execution

### Challenge
Many consumers don't know where they stand with their credit or how to improve it. While Credit.com provided helpful resources, its user experience needed to evolve to better support a growing base of financially curious, digitally savvy users. The team also saw an opportunity to introduce a new product tier — ExtraCredit — to offer more robust tools for users ready to take the next step in their credit journey.

### Research & Insights
User interviews and data analysis revealed key opportunities:
- **Information Overload** — Users wanted a simple way to understand their credit status without digging through complex reports.
- **Lack of Direction** — Many didn't know what steps to take to improve their score after viewing it.
- **Demand for More Advanced Tools** — Power users wanted additional services like identity protection, credit building tools, and more frequent score updates — without switching to a competitor.

These insights laid the groundwork for simplifying the core experience and introducing the ExtraCredit product.

### Key Improvements
- **Streamlined Credit Report Card UX** — Redesigned to surface actionable insights and reduce cognitive load. Users could quickly see their score, what affected it, and what to do next.
- **Clearer Onboarding & Progress Indicators** — Helped new users understand the value proposition and start seeing progress sooner.
- **Free Credit Score Access** — Refined flows and CTAs to boost engagement and retention for returning users.
- **ExtraCredit Product Tier** — Designed and launched the premium experience offering five core benefits:
  - *Track It* — Daily credit report monitoring
  - *Build It* — Rent and utility payment reporting
  - *Reward It* — Cashback offers based on financial habits
  - *Guard It* — Identity theft protection
  - *Restore It* — Credit repair partner integrations
- **Responsive Web Design** — Ensured seamless experiences across mobile and desktop for all user tiers.

### Outcome
The redesign and product expansion significantly improved user engagement and broadened Credit.com's appeal to a wider audience:
- Increased activation and retention for free users engaging with their credit score and report card
- Successful launch of ExtraCredit, with strong early adoption and upsell performance
- Improved user satisfaction, reflected in feedback and behavior metrics post-launch

### Conclusion
My work at Credit.com helped evolve the platform from a static score-checking tool to a more comprehensive, action-oriented hub for financial empowerment. By leading the UX and product design across both free and premium tiers, I helped create a scalable and supportive experience for users at every stage of their credit journey.
